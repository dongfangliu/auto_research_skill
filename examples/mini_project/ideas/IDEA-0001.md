## Raw Idea

Use structured research cards to help agents recover context after interruptions and continue research without relying on a single conversation history.

## Why It Matters

Research work is nonlinear. If agents can resume from files instead of memory, they may support longer projects with less context loss.

## Possible Contribution

A lightweight workflow for preserving research intent, decisions, evidence, and claims across interruptions.

## Counterarguments

- Markdown structure may add overhead.
- Agents might still miss important linked files.
- The value may come from disciplined documentation rather than agent-specific design.

## Literature Questions

- What prior systems use external memory for long-running research or agent workflows?
- What metrics are used to evaluate task resumption quality?

## Minimum Verification Path

Run a mock interruption-recovery task and see whether an agent can resume from `STATE.md` and linked cards.
